About me

I am a PhD candidate in Northwestern's Technology and Social Behavior program, working with Prof. Haoqi Zhang at the Delta Lab. My research supports people’s ability to interface with existing artificial intelligence (AI) capabilities. I build human-AI interfaces to effectively ensure AI-enabled applications support people's desires for socially connecting and personally empowering experiences.

In my dissertation, I designed a platform for creating Opportunistic Collective Experiences (OCEs), a new AI-enabled social technology that identifies and facilitates opportunities for friends and family to engage in shared experiences. To create OCEs, I built tools for interfacing with existing AI detection and coordination abilities, so designers can structure opportunities, express high-level situations, and encode human expectations for execution during deployment.

In my work at Google Research, I developed steering interfaces so creators could feel greater control and effectively evoke emotions in music when partnering with generative AI. Check out the blog post I wrote that summarizes this People First Approach to developing Generative AI applications for music creation.
